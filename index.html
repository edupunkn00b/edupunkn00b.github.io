Remus King (author)

From Fic-ipedia, the faux encyclopedia for fics

This article is about Remus King, the author. For other uses, see Remus (disambiguation).

Remus King, Halloween, 2019

Remus King (born March 15, 1989) is an American author of horror, crime, suspense, and supernatural comedic novels. His books have sold over 50 million copies, and have a rabid fanbase. In addition to over a dozen novels, King has published several short story collections, and three volumes of dark gothic poetry.

In addition to his writing, King is a gay rights activist and in 2008 was arrested outside an illegal gathering in front of the United Nations, protesting the treatment of LGBTQ youth throughout the world, including in the United States. He is an ardent opponent of so-called "conversion therapy," which he likens to medieval torture methods, and has frequently advocated for a revision on the DSM-V and its continued relegation of gender and sexuality in the "disorders" section. (see Controversy).

King regularly speaks at fundraising events supporting True Colors, The Trevor Project and It Gets Better. He has been repeatedly hailed by several up-and-coming young authors as a major inspiration for their own writing and lives.
